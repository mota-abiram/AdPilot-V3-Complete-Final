# Creative & Video Creation SOP

Meta Lead Gen primary; also applies to Google Demand Gen.

## 0. Scope & Formats

Static sizes to produce on every concept:

- `1080x1080`
- `1080x1920`
- `1200x628`
- `960x1200`

Rules:

- Always design per size. Do not rely on auto-cropping.
- Video ratios: `1:1`, `4:5`, `9:16`.
- Reels and Stories are priority.
- Mobile-first and native-looking ads win.
- Create separate creative adaptations for Reel and Story sizes.

Reference note:

- Use the provided reference images when building Meta Reel and Story adaptations.

## 1. Creative Brief

Fill this before production:

- Project facts: price bands, BHK, SFT range, key quantified USPs, commute minutes, RERA or equivalent details.
- Offer: current promo, urgency, deadline, limited inventory.
- Audience: exact buyer segment.
- Tone: polished brand vs relatable/raw.
- Default to raw if the brand allows it.
- Compliance: avoid policy-risk elements.
- Deliverables: static in all sizes, videos `35-50s` plus `6s` bumper, captions, thumbnails.

## 2. Creative Principles

### Price + Place + Proof on-frame

Put these directly on the creative:

- Price or price band
- Location
- `1-3` quantified USPs
- Detailed configuration where relevant

### Highlight the Core Offering

- The product or service being sold must be instantly obvious.

### Urgency & FOMO

- Use real deadlines and real scarcity.
- Refresh offers periodically.
- Make urgency visible on-frame.

### Show Benefits & the Problem

- Show the outcome clearly.
- Or lead with the cost of delaying.
- Make the problem feel important before positioning the offer as relief.

### Relatable Beats Over-produced

- Raw, native, less ad-like creative often performs better.
- Instagram-style typography and realistic imagery are encouraged when the brand allows it.

### Social Proof

- Testimonials
- Influencer content
- UGC

### Pattern Interrupts

- Collages
- Sequential carousels using AIDA
- Instant Experience where suitable

## 3. Static Design Rules

Apply on every image:

- Avoid Facebook-like blue/white-heavy layouts.
- Prefer contrasting colors like yellow, neon green, crimson, orange, magenta.
- Use green ticks for benefits where suitable.
- Do not use arrows, mouse cursors, or fake play buttons.
- When using faces, zoom toward the eyes if it fits the concept.
- Consider simple Canva-style collages.
- Consider sequential carousel storytelling.
- Consider Instant Experience for deeper on-platform education.

## 4. Policy & Sanity

Allowed and effective:

- Green ticks
- Bold offers
- On-frame price
- Testimonials without exaggerated or unsubstantiated claims

Avoid:

- Arrows
- Mouse cursors
- Fake UI
- Fake play icons
- Claim overreach

Always keep sizes native.

## 5. Video Creation Rules

Video length:

- Primary: `35-50s`

Core rules:

- Make it feel like a Reel.
- Lead with movement in `<=3s`.
- First 3 words must address the target audience or buyer persona.
- Hook in the first 3 seconds.
- Use pattern interrupts like VCR/reverse, jump cuts, B-roll, mid-roll switches.
- Use a strong top headline.
- Use captions always.
- Optional progress bar is encouraged.
- Follow AIDA: attention, problem/benefit, outcome, CTA + FOMO.
- Make videos mute-friendly.

## 6. Asset Checklist

### Static

- Price band and location on-frame
- `3-5` benefits
- One clear CTA
- One FOMO variant
- One social-proof variant
- Color contrast checked
- No arrows, cursors, or fake play buttons

### Video

- Thumbnail with price, location, and USP
- First 3 words address TG or buyer persona
- Hook in `<=3s`
- Top-bar headline
- Captions
- Optional progress bar
- CTA end-card aligned to LP/form CTA
- Pattern interrupts included

## 7. Build & Upload

### Meta

- One concept per ad set
- Advantage+ ON for prospecting
- Upload all sizes
- Map copy variants properly

### Google Demand Gen

- Upload `1:1`, `9:16`, and `1.91:1` / `1200x628`
- Keep on-frame messaging aligned with Meta where possible
- Add long headlines and descriptions

## 8. Diagnostics & Success Metrics

Diagnosis order:

1. First-frame rate
2. Thumb-stop rate
3. Hold `3s -> 15s`
4. Link CTR
5. CPM with weak CTR

Targets:

- First-frame rate: `>=90%`
- Thumb-stop: `>=30%`
- Hold through the middle: aim `>=35%`

Fixes:

- Low first-frame rate: thumbnail, first 3 words, contrast
- Low thumb-stop: hook, thumbnail, contrast
- Weak hold: tighten middle, sharpen messaging, add interrupts
- Weak CTR: sharpen CTA, offer, benefits
- High CPM + weak CTR: creative/audience mismatch or fatigue

## 9. Refresh & Revive Playbook

- If performance dips, wait `3-5` days.
- If it does not recover, refresh creative.
- Fatigue signs:
- Frequency rising
- CTR falling
- CPM rising
- Approx frequency thresholds:
- `>2.5` for TOFU/MOFU
- `>4` for BOFU
- Clone winning creatives from other campaigns before creating net-new.
- Minor frame tweaks or light animation can help reset performance.

## 10. Production Guardrails

- One core idea per creative.
- Do not cram price, proof, urgency, social proof, and every USP into one frame.
- Multiple placements require multiple dimensions.
- Keep one experiment campaign always on for next-gen winners.

## 11. BOFU Rules

- Answer objections in the creative.
- Use testimonials, walkthroughs, and price-reveal hooks.
- Exclude recent leads and bookings.
- Keep BOFU pools distinct to reduce overlap.

## 12. Handover Checklist

- Brief approved
- Static exports done in all required sizes
- Videos in `6s`, `15s`, `30s` where needed, with `<=3s` hook, captions, CTA end-card
- No-go elements removed
- FOMO and social-proof variants included
- File names versioned and archived
- Uploaded to Meta and Google DG
- Reporting preset ready for thumb-stop, hold, CTR, CPM, CPC
- Refresh window set for `14-21` days or earlier if fatigue appears

## Notes Bridging to Optimization SOPs

- When CPM rises and CTR falls, treat it as fatigue and refresh creative.
- Keep audience exclusions clean across funnel layers to reduce auction overlap.
